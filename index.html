Corinne Riddell. Social and Perinatal Epidemiologist

I am a social and perinatal epidemiologist and post doctoral researcher at McGill University, based in Montreal. My current research focus is health inequalities between racial, ethnic, and socioeconomic groups. I have spent the past year developing a web application to understand why blacks have a shorter life expectancy than whites in the United States (app, working paper, GitHub repository). I have also investigated differences in trends in US infant mortality by race (GitHub repository) and in quality of obstetric care between First Nations and non-First Nations women in British Columbia, Canada (paper). My PhD dissertation examined variation in obstetrical practice, especially in the use of cesarean delivery, across hospitals in Canada and the US.

Beyond my interests in social epidemiology, I love creating data visualizations and data tools more broadly. Some of my recent projects include:

  • A sample size calculator to help researchers calculate sample size in studies with repeated measurements (i.e., panel data) and continuous outcomes.
  • e-value calculator for easy e-value calculation, a measure of confounding bias neccesary to nullify an estimated effect in an observational study.
  • A report of US state-level trends in drug overdose mortality since 1979.
